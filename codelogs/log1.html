Code Log 1: HTML Semantic Tags

Semantic tags in HTML are specfic tags meant to convey what they contain with more accuracy than just the <p> tag. At first, these tags are indistigushable from each other, but this can be changed with CSS. It's up to the coder to decide how they look. They can also be utlized by screen readers.

A nonsemantic HTML tag is a tag that cannot be utlized by a screen reader, and does not define a specifc layout of the page. examples of these are the <div> and <span> tags.

The benefits of semantic tags over nonsemantic tags are their compatability with screen readers, and ease of coding as you can easily tell exactly what information belongs in what section, and what the purpose of each section is.
